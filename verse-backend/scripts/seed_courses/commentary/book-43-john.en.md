# John

## Introduction to John

The Gospel of John is the fourth Gospel, written by John, the disciple whom Jesus loved. While the other three Gospels (Matthew, Mark, and Luke) recount the life and teaching of Jesus from a broadly parallel viewpoint, John concentrates, from a deeper theological meditation, on the question, "Who is Jesus?" John states his purpose plainly: "But these are written, that ye might believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God; and that believing ye might have life through his name" (20:31).

This Gospel opens with the majestic declaration, "In the beginning was the Word." It deliberately echoes the "In the beginning" of Genesis and proclaims that Jesus is the eternal Word of God who existed before creation. Through the seven signs (miracles) Jesus performs and the seven "I am" declarations, John steadily unveils His divinity and His mission.

The Gospel of John is deep and yet simple. It is written in language plain enough for a child to read, yet within it lies a spring of truth that never runs dry though meditated upon for a lifetime. For this reason, from ancient times many have read this Gospel as their first step into faith.

## The Heart of the Message

**The sovereign grace of God** is seen nowhere more clearly than in the Gospel of John. It is not that a person comes to faith on his own; rather, Jesus says, "No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him" (6:44). Salvation is, from beginning to end, a work that God begins and completes. His word to Nicodemus, that one "must be born again," likewise teaches that new life is not the resolve of man but the work of the Spirit coming from above.

At the same time, the Gospel of John testifies to **the glory of God the Son**. The Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we beheld His glory. Jesus is not merely a great teacher but the true God, one in essence with the Father. Yet that glory shines most brightly at the cross. For John, Jesus being "lifted up" is at once His being crucified and His being exalted to heaven, and it is precisely there that the love of God is brought to completion.

Above all, the Gospel of John is a book of **faith and life**. Here, "eternal life" is not merely life after death but a new existence that begins here and now in knowing God. To believe in Jesus is to be united with Him as one and to live as a branch of the true vine, and only within this union can a person bear true fruit. This life, begun in grace, can never be taken away.

## Recommended Verses to Memorize

> In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.

**John 1:1**
A majestic prologue proclaiming the eternal divinity of Jesus. To engrave this verse is to hold unshakably that Jesus is not a creature but the Creator God.

> And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth.

**John 1:14**
A verse that holds the mystery of the incarnation. The truth that the eternal God came clothed in a body like ours keeps us ever mindful of how near God has come to us.

> For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.

**John 3:16**
The most beloved verse, compressing the gospel of the whole Bible into a single sentence. God's love and the way of salvation are held in this one verse, making it a firm rock whenever we need the assurance of faith.

> And ye shall know the truth, and the truth shall make you free.

**John 8:32**
This teaches that the source of true freedom is found in Jesus, who is the truth. It holds the heart to live freely in the truth, unbound by the lies and fears of the world.

> I am the resurrection, and the life: he that believeth in me, though he were dead, yet shall he live.

**John 11:25**
A verse proclaiming the authority of Jesus who conquered death. In the face of grief over a lost loved one or fear of death, it becomes a comfort that lets us grasp the hope of resurrection.

> Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me.

**John 14:6**
A verse that plainly declares Jesus to be the only way of salvation. It centers our faith upon Christ alone and offers a firm direction in a shaking age.

> I am the vine, ye are the branches: He that abideth in me, and I in him, the same bringeth forth much fruit: for without me ye can do nothing.

**John 15:5**
This teaches that union with Christ is the whole of the life of faith. The truth that we bear fruit not by our own strength but by abiding in Him leads us into a life of humility and dependence.

## Why It Is Good to Memorize This Book

Memorizing the verses of the Gospel of John is a work of engraving deep in the heart who Jesus is. The words of this Gospel vividly hold the divinity of Jesus, the love of the incarnation, the way of salvation, and the true life lived in union with Him. Because John conveys deep truth in simple language, the memorized verses become a key that builds the believer's assurance and opens the heart's door of the seeker. When these words take root in the heart, then amid any doubt or trial you can answer the question "In whom do I believe?" without wavering.

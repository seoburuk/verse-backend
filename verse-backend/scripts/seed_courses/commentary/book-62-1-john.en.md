# 1 John

First John was written by the apostle John, likely from Ephesus, to churches in Asia Minor troubled by teachers who denied that Jesus had truly come in the flesh and by a creeping indifference toward sin among those who claimed to know God. John writes as an old man who had walked with Christ himself, and his tone throughout is that of a father testing whether his children's faith is genuine.

The letter moves in repeating cycles rather than a straight argument: walking in the light, loving the brethren, refusing to love the world, watching for antichrists, and resting in the love of God that produces obedience. Instead of laying out abstract doctrine, John keeps circling back to one test — does the life match the claim.

## Core Message

First John refuses to accept cheap professions of faith. Anyone who says "I know him" while disregarding his commandments is called a liar (2:4). Salvation is not merely a past decision but a present reality confirmed by ongoing obedience and love. This is not a claim that works earn salvation, but that true new birth inevitably bears fruit that can be seen.

At the same time, John does not pretend believers reach sinless perfection. "If we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves" (1:8) shows that honest confession and continual repentance, not flawlessness, are the normal pattern of a believer's life. The promise that Christ's blood keeps cleansing those who confess makes clear that assurance rests not on self-righteousness but solely on the atoning work of Christ.

John defines love not as sentiment but as truth put into action (3:18). The order in 4:19 — that we love because he first loved us — insists that love does not originate with us; sovereign grace is the sole wellspring of every genuine act of love and obedience.

## Verses Worth Memorizing

> If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. (1 John 1:9)

This verse opens a path of confession rather than despair for the believer who has sinned. Assurance rests not on sinlessness but on God's faithfulness and justice.

> Behold, what manner of love the Father hath bestowed upon us, that we should be called the sons of God. (1 John 3:1)

Being called children of God is not something earned but a gift of the Father's freely bestowed love.

> He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love. (1 John 4:7-8, excerpt)

Knowledge of God and the practice of love can never be separated — the heart of the whole letter in a single line.

> We love him, because he first loved us. (1 John 4:19)

A short, forceful statement that the origin of all genuine love lies not in us but in God alone.

> And this is the record, that God hath given to us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. (1 John 5:11-13, excerpt)

Assurance of eternal life rests not on feeling or experience but on the objective fact that this life is found in the Son.

## Why It's Worth Memorizing

The verses of 1 John give a struggling believer both self-examination and assurance in the same breath. Memorizing them keeps faith from settling into mere profession, pushing it instead toward love in action, while also engraving on the heart the path back to Christ's blood every time we stumble.

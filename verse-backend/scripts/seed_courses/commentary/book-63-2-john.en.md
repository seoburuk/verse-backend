# 2 John

Second John is one of the shortest letters in the New Testament, written by the apostle John, calling himself "the elder," to "the elect lady and her children" — likely either a specific woman or, more probably, a symbolic reference to a local church and its members. Though brief, the letter carries the same urgency found in 1 John: a warning against false teachers who denied the true incarnation of Christ.

In a few short verses, John sets love and truth side by side. Love cannot exist apart from truth, and truth is empty unless it is lived out in love — this is the backbone of the letter. What looks like a simple matter of hospitality toward traveling teachers becomes a firm, practical instruction not to welcome those who bring false doctrine into the home.

## Core Message

Second John firmly rejects the idea that truth can be sacrificed in the name of love. Genuine love always walks in truth and according to truth (1:4-6). The declaration that whoever does not abide in the doctrine of Christ does not have God (1:9) shows that doctrinal indifference or indiscriminate tolerance can never be justified under the banner of love.

The letter warns that even receiving or greeting a false teacher makes one a partaker in his evil deeds (1:10-11). This is not coldness or exclusivism but flows from the conviction that truth about the person and work of Christ is bound up with the very survival of the church. Love does not bend truth to accommodate tolerance; it holds fellow believers fast within the truth.

## Verses Worth Memorizing

> And this is love, that we walk after his commandments. This is the commandment, That, as ye have heard from the beginning, ye should walk in it. (2 John 1:6)

Love is not confirmed by feeling or good intentions alone but by the concrete steps of obedience taken in following Christ's commandments.

## Why It's Worth Memorizing

This short verse fixes in the mind, briefly and clearly, that love and obedience can never be separated. Whenever the temptation arises to blur the truth in the name of relationship or tolerance, it recalls that genuine love means walking within the commandments.

# 2 Peter

Second Peter is, in effect, the apostle Peter's last testament, written knowing his death was near. He warns of false teachers already infiltrating the church and urges believers to stand firmly on unshaken true knowledge and moral maturity. In particular, he confronts false teaching that mocks the promise of Christ's return in order to justify a lazy and dissolute life, insisting that God's promise has neither been delayed nor abandoned.

Though short, this letter addresses both the foundation of faith and hope for the future together. Peter reminds his readers that he was an eyewitness of Christ's glory on the mountain, and affirms that Scripture did not originate from the will of man but came from God through the moving of the Holy Spirit. The purpose of the book is to make clear the true foundation of faith in an age flooded with false teaching.

## Core Message

What 2 Peter emphasizes is that the Christian life is not a one-time decision but demands ongoing growth. The declaration that God's divine power has already given us all things pertaining to life and godliness shows that spiritual growth is not achieved by our own effort alone but rests on grace already given. Yet this grace can never be an excuse for laziness — it must lead instead to a diligent life that adds virtue to faith and knowledge to virtue.

Peter also vigorously defends the divine authority of Scripture. His declaration that no prophecy ever came by the will of man, but that holy men of God spoke as they were moved by the Holy Spirit, nails down that Scripture is not human religious speculation but certain divine revelation. Only on this view of Scripture can believers stand unshaken against the plausible words of false teachers. What looks like a delay in Christ's return is in fact God's longsuffering, an expression of mercy that desires even one more soul to come to repentance — a point this book makes unmistakably clear.

## Verses Worth Memorizing

> According as his divine power hath given unto us all things that pertain unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue — 2 Peter 1:3

This verse affirms that the source of a godly life is not our own willpower but God's divine power already given to us.

> Knowing this first, that no prophecy of the scripture is of any private interpretation. For the prophecy came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost. — 2 Peter 1:20-21

This verse firmly establishes that Scripture is not a human creation but the word of God given through the moving of the Spirit, forming the basis for the inerrancy of Scripture.

> The Lord is not slack concerning his promise, as some men count slackness; but is longsuffering to us-ward, not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance. — 2 Peter 3:9

This verse teaches that the time that seems to delay Christ's return is not God's inability but His merciful longsuffering, widening the opportunity to repent.

## Why It's Worth Memorizing

Memorizing the verses of 2 Peter firmly establishes in the heart the certain authority of Scripture and the faithfulness of God's promises amid an unstable age. In a world overflowing with false teaching and mockery, these verses give a clear grip on what we stand on and what hope we are to live by.
